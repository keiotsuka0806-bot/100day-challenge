# VinRoute Core MVP

## One-line pitch
VinRoute is a route-branching collection game where players roll dice, choose paths, claim region cards first, and complete sets to build the highest-value collection.

## 3-minute rules

### 1. Who the player is
The player is a wine collector traveling across routes to complete the strongest collection.

### 2. What the player does on a turn
1. Roll the dice.
2. Move one step at a time.
3. At each step, choose one of the branch routes.
4. When you land on a region, claim it if nobody has claimed it yet.
5. If the region is already claimed, you get only a small travel bonus.
6. Finish the turn after spending all movement.

### 3. Why movement matters
Movement is the main decision. The player must choose which branch to take, because the route determines which cards can be claimed next.

### 4. Why collecting matters
Each claimed region adds collection value. Completing a region set gives a big bonus.

### 5. Why competition matters
Only the first player to claim a region gets its full value. Other players must choose a different route or settle for a small bonus. This creates race, denial, and timing.

### 6. Why knowledge matters
Knowledge is not the win condition. Quiz and market effects are helper systems that reveal better routes or improve future choices.

### 7. How the game ends
After the final season, the player with the highest collection value wins.

## MVP core
- Dice movement
- Branching route choices
- Region card collection
- Set completion bonus
- First-come competition
- Light quiz and market hints as optional flavor

## MVP exclusions
- Complex construction
- Complex resource economy
- Complex brewing loop
- World map at full scale
- Heavy AI

## Design intent
The game should be readable in one sentence:
"Roll, choose a route, claim regions first, complete sets, win with the strongest collection."
